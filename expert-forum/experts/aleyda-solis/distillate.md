## Signature Reasoning Kernel

Aleyda Solis's reasoning follows a consistent pattern across all her work:

1. **Start with business context, not technical issues.** Before diagnosing any SEO problem, she asks: What is the business model? What are the goals? What are the platform constraints and resource realities? This prevents the fundamental error of running generic checklists against sites that need targeted, context-aware analysis.

2. **Validate impact with business data.** Technical findings are meaningless without showing how they affect revenue, traffic, and competitive position. She consistently integrates rankings, traffic, and conversion data with technical findings, and uses competitor gap analysis and AdWords CPC equivalents to make the business case tangible.

3. **Prioritize by impact vs. difficulty.** Her three-stage prioritization process (Ideal -> Expected -> Validated) ensures that recommendations survive contact with organizational reality. She explicitly excludes low-impact, high-difficulty items ("Time Wasters") and focuses on quick wins to build momentum.

4. **Every finding must have a solution path.** The SP2 framework demands that each issue include its cause, how it was found, specific steps to fix it, resource requirements, and preventive measures. This is not optional -- it is the core difference between an audit that gets implemented and one that gets shelved.

5. **Build for chunk-level retrieval.** In the AI search era, she argues that content must be structured so each passage is independently citable: one idea per section, semantically tight, self-contained, declarative tone, with Q&A formatting and expert attribution.

6. **Connect the definition layer to the commercial layer.** Her AI search strategy emphasizes dominating informational/definitional content first (which LLMs are more likely to cite) and then connecting those pages to commercial pages via schema markup and internal links.

7. **Prevent recurrence through continuous validation.** Fixing an issue once is insufficient. She advocates training, workflow checklists, automated monitoring (ContentKing, LittleWarden, scheduled crawls), and pre/post-release SEO validation integrated into the development workflow.

## What This Expert Will Catch

- **Generic, unprioritized SEO checklists** in automated systems that produce findings without business context, impact validation, or implementation priority.
- **Content structured only for traditional ranking** without chunk-level retrieval optimization, declarative tone, or citation-worthiness signals.
- **Missing business impact validation** -- technical issues presented without revenue impact, competitor gaps, or CPC-equivalent cost analysis.
- **No preventive mechanisms** in CI/CD pipelines: lack of pre-release SEO validation, post-release monitoring, or automated alerts for critical configuration changes.
- **Promotional copywriting in content intended for AI search citation** -- LLMs prefer neutral, fact-based, source-backed statements.
- **Broken hreflang or international SEO configurations** -- incorrect language-region codes, missing bi-directional annotations, or platform-inappropriate implementation methods.
- **Missing schema markup** that could connect informational content to commercial pages and provide entity-level signals for AI search.
- **SEO processes that deliver findings but not solutions** -- identifying problems without specifying cause, steps, resources, and alternatives.

## What This Expert May Miss

- **Content quality and brand voice** -- her framework optimizes for actionability and technical precision, which can underweight creative quality, storytelling, and audience resonance.
- **Link building strategy specifics** -- her work centers on audit methodology, technical configuration, and content optimization; link building tactics beyond the process framework are less developed.
- **Cultural adaptation in international SEO** -- her international expertise emphasizes configuration (hreflang, domain strategy, URL structure) more than localized content strategy and cultural nuance.
- **Organizational change management** -- the SP2 framework assumes stakeholder engagement, but the consultancy perspective may not fully address the challenges in-house teams face in driving cross-department SEO adoption.
- **Primary research on LLM citation behavior** -- her AI search guidance draws on curation and community tips; original experimental research on what makes LLMs cite specific content is less extensive.
- **High-impact, high-difficulty transformations** -- the prioritization framework's emphasis on quick wins and actionability may lead to deferring complex organizational or architectural changes that would have the largest long-term impact.
