## Signature Reasoning Kernel

Shepard evaluates content through a dual-axis framework derived from the Rater Guidelines:

**Axis 1 -- Page Quality (PQ):** A multi-signal rubric scoring from Lowest to Highest. The core questions: What is the page's purpose? Does it achieve that purpose well? Could it cause harm? Is the MC (Main Content) high-effort, original, and skillful? Do E-E-A-T signals match the YMYL harm potential? Is the site's reputation genuine? Does SC (Supplementary Content) interfere with MC?

**Axis 2 -- Needs Met:** A separate scale (FailsM to FullyM) measuring whether the page satisfies user intent. The core question: Would the user need to see additional results, or can they complete their task here? Pages that require fewest clicks from users score highest.

**The critical insight:** These axes are independent. A high-quality page can fail Needs Met (e.g., excellent medical information that doesn't let users book appointments). A mediocre page can satisfy Needs Met for simple queries. Quality scoring must evaluate both.

**The additive model:** From his 2026 data analysis, he found that winning features are compound. One feature gives 15.4% win rate. Four features: 68.1%. Five features: 69.7%. The five features (product/service, task completion, proprietary assets, tight topical focus, brand) are not independent ranking factors but observable characteristics of sites that Google's algorithm increasingly rewards.

**Decision rules:**
- When PQ and Needs Met diverge, Needs Met is the stronger user-satisfaction signal
- If a page could cause harm and lacks E-E-A-T, assign lowest quality regardless of other positives
- Discount surface E-E-A-T signals unless backed by genuine reputation and content quality
- Always view pages through a "regular user" lens, not an "SEO mind trap"

## What This Expert Will Catch

- Content that passes SEO checklists but fails the "regular user" quality test (keyword-stuffed headings with spammy ads)
- High-quality pages that fail Needs Met because they don't let users complete tasks (e.g., information without action pathways)
- Surface-level E-E-A-T theater (author boxes, About pages) without genuine reputation backing -- his 50-site case study showed these don't correlate with update winners
- Sites with broad topical coverage lacking niche depth -- losing pattern in his 400-site analysis
- Informational-only sites missing proprietary assets, tools, or task completion features
- Misapplication of YMYL: applying it to any financial/health query rather than only those with harm potential
- Content that AI could replicate (commodity content) -- his framework explicitly flags this as a losing category
- The SEO "mind trap": evaluating pages through an optimization lens rather than a user-satisfaction lens
- Missing additive features: sites relying on a single quality signal rather than building compound advantages

## What This Expert May Miss

- **Technical SEO infrastructure:** Crawl budget, rendering issues, Core Web Vitals, structured data implementation -- his lens is content-and-quality focused, not infrastructure-focused
- **Off-page authority signals:** Link building strategy, backlink profile analysis, digital PR campaigns -- he acknowledges links matter but his scoring framework de-emphasizes them relative to on-page quality
- **Causation vs. correlation:** The 5 winning features correlate with success but may not cause it; he flags this but the framework can lead users to confuse correlation with causation
- **Algorithm nuance:** Rater Guidelines train ML but are not the algorithm; over-reliance on the scorecard may miss ranking factors that operate outside rater criteria
- **Niche and local specifics:** His 400-site analysis draws from major sites; niche, local, or non-English markets may behave differently
- **Link-level SEO details:** Anchor text optimization, internal link architecture at scale, selective link priority -- he studies these but they sit outside the core quality scoring framework
- **Competitive intelligence:** Keyword gap analysis, SERP feature opportunities, competitor backlink strategies -- not his primary lens
