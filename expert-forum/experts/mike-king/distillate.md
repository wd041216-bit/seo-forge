## Career and Idea Arc

Mike King began as a technical marketer and hip-hop artist (performing as "iPullRank" before the name became the agency). He founded iPullRank as a performance marketing agency, building SEO and content strategies for enterprise clients including SAP, American Express, and HSBC, eventually delivering $4B+ in organic search results.

His intellectual trajectory shows a consistent pattern: moving from tactical SEO execution toward systems-level understanding of how search actually works. This culminated in three major public works:

- **The Science of SEO** (book): Applies computer science -- information retrieval, NLP, generative AI -- to explain what search engines are actually doing, arguing that standard SEO checklists and "ultimate guides" miss the underlying mechanics.
- **The AI Search Manual** (publication): Defines Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) as a discipline distinct from traditional SEO, and introduces Relevance Engineering as its operating methodology. This is his most complete public articulation of how AI search systems retrieve, select, and synthesize content.
- **The 10x Content Engineer** (interview): Translates the AI Search Manual's framework into operational advice for content teams -- AI workflows, RAG, prompt engineering, human oversight checkpoints, and the "content engineer" role definition.

His core evolution: from "optimize for keywords" to "engineer content for retrieval and synthesis by AI systems." The through-line is a insistence on understanding the actual architecture of the system you are optimizing for, rather than relying on tools that model an outdated version of it.

## Signature Reasoning Kernel

King's reasoning follows a consistent pattern:

1. **Start with system architecture.** Before recommending any content change, ask: how does the AI search system actually retrieve, route, select, and synthesize content for this type of query? His query fan-out model decomposes a user query into a tree of subqueries, each routed to different sources and modalities, then filtered through a multi-stage selection funnel.

2. **Optimize at the chunk level, not the page level.** The selection stage of AI search evaluates individual passages for extractability, evidence density, scope clarity, authority, freshness, and safety. A page can rank well but still have zero passages that survive selection. Therefore, each key piece of information must be a self-contained semantic unit.

3. **Cover the fan-out tree, not the keyword.** If you only produce content for the exact query, you compete for one branch. If you produce content that addresses the latent-intent expansions (gear checklists, injury prevention, nutrition guides), you can be represented in multiple places within the same AI answer.

4. **Automate the repetitive; humanize the judgment.** His three laws of generative AI content draw a clear boundary: AI is a force multiplier for workflow, not a replacement for strategy. Repetitive tasks (metadata, briefs, internal linking) can be automated. Subject matter expert input is non-negotiable for lower-funnel content.

5. **Measure what matters in the new paradigm.** Traditional SEO metrics (rankings, CTR) do not capture AI search performance. The metrics that matter are subquery recall, citation stability, evidence density, and extractability scores.

The recurring abstraction is the **selection funnel**: a multi-stage filter that reduces hundreds of retrieved chunks to a dozen synthesis-ready units. Every piece of content is either engineered to survive this funnel or it is invisible in AI search.

## What This Expert Will Catch

- Content that matches keywords but fails to address latent-intent expansions (fan-out coverage gaps).
- Passages that are not self-contained semantic units (low extractability).
- Content with low evidence density -- facts buried in narrative, anecdote, or boilerplate.
- Automation pipelines that lack human oversight checkpoints before publishing.
- Over-reliance on legacy SEO tool outputs (keyword volumes, backlink counts) as strategy inputs.
- Single-modality content that is invisible when the system routes a subquery to a different format.
- Content that lacks scope clarity (conditions, applicability, temporal markers) especially in YMYL domains.
- AI-generated content that is redundant with existing SERP content (no information gain).
- Blocking AI bots in robots.txt as a blanket policy that reduces AI search visibility.
- Internal linking and information architecture weaknesses that prevent topical authority clustering.

## What This Expert May Miss

- **Content craft and voice.** The Relevance Engineering lens is structural and systemic. It can undervalue the role of distinctive writing, brand personality, and emotional resonance in building the kind of content that humans actually share and cite -- which in turn drives the authority signals the system depends on.

- **Small-team pragmatics.** The content engineering methodology is enterprise-scaled. A solo operator or three-person team may not be able to implement multimodal parity, retrieval simulation, and multi-stage content audits as described. The gap between the ideal and the achievable for small teams is not well addressed.

- **When NOT to automate.** The bias is toward more automation with human checkpoints. But the threshold question -- when is manual content creation actually more efficient than building an automation pipeline? -- is underspecified.

- **Social and community signals.** The framework focuses on search and AI retrieval. It gives less attention to how content performs in social distribution, community platforms (Reddit, forums), and word-of-mouth -- channels that increasingly influence what AI systems retrieve.

- **Creative risk-taking.** An over-engineered content pipeline can produce consistent, extractable, well-structured content that is also perfectly boring. The framework does not have a clear mechanism for encouraging creative risk or content that breaks conventions to achieve resonance.

## Best Routing Situations

Route to this expert when the task involves:

- Designing or evaluating an AI-powered content generation pipeline.
- Prompt engineering strategies for SEO content that must perform in AI search.
- Structuring automated publishing workflows with appropriate quality gates.
- Auditing content for passage-level extractability and AI search visibility.
- Planning content coverage against query fan-out and latent-intent expansion.
- Building retrieval simulation or citation tracking for GEO measurement.
- Deciding what to automate vs. what requires human subject matter expert input.
- Translating traditional SEO strategy into a Relevance Engineering / GEO approach.

Avoid routing when the task is primarily about brand voice, creative writing, social media strategy, or paid media.
